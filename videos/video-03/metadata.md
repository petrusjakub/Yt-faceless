CHANNEL: WealthMind Academy
STYLE: Faceless YouTube, educational, conversational but authoritative
TONE: Approachable expert - like a smart friend revealing wealth secrets over coffee. Not preachy, not judgmental toward "broke" people. Data-driven, motivational, and empowering. The goal is to show that wealth is built through daily decisions, not luck.
TARGET AUDIENCE: English-speaking young adults (18-35) who want to build wealth but don't know where to start. They may be living paycheck to paycheck and want practical, proven habits they can adopt immediately.

VIDEO STRUCTURE (10-12 minutes):

1. HOOK (0:00-0:45)
- Open with a surprising statistic: "A study of 233 wealthy individuals found that 72% of the rich had at least one specific daily habit that the broke never practiced."
- Challenge the assumption: "It's not about how much you earn - it's about what you do with every dollar."
- Promise: by the end, viewers will know the exact 7 habits and can start implementing them TODAY
- Mention research backing: Thomas Corley's 5-year Rich Habits Study, National Study of Millionaires

2. HABIT #1: PAY YOURSELF FIRST - AUTOMATE SAVINGS (0:45-2:00)
- Rich habit: Automatically transfer 20%+ of income to savings/investments BEFORE spending
- Broke habit: Save whatever is "left over" at end of month (which is usually nothing)
- Research: 94% of millionaires live on less than they make; they automate first
- The psychology: willpower is finite, automation removes the decision
- Example: Someone earning $4,000/month who automates $800 on payday vs. someone who "tries to save" and consistently fails
- Actionable step: Set up automatic transfer TODAY, even if it's just $50

3. HABIT #2: LIVE BELOW YOUR MEANS, NOT AT YOUR MEANS (2:00-3:15)
- Rich habit: Spending significantly less than income regardless of how much they earn
- Broke habit: Spending rises to match (or exceed) every raise or bonus
- Research: The average millionaire drives a car that's 3+ years old (Thomas Stanley, The Millionaire Next Door)
- Key insight: The difference between "can afford" and "should buy"
- Statistic: 74% of millionaires in the National Study of Millionaires have never had a car payment over $600/month
- Example: A doctor earning $300k living on $80k vs. a middle-class family earning $80k spending $85k
- Actionable step: After your next raise, save 50% of the increase automatically

4. HABIT #3: INVEST CONSISTENTLY, NOT JUST SAVE (3:15-4:30)
- Rich habit: Put money into appreciating assets (stocks, index funds, real estate) every single month
- Broke habit: Keep all money in a checking/savings account losing value to inflation
- Research: 75% of self-made millionaires invested consistently for 20+ years
- Key insight: Saving alone doesn't build wealth - your money must work for you
- Compound interest example: $300/month invested at 10% average return = $678,000 in 30 years
- The inflation problem: $10,000 in savings loses ~$300-$700 in purchasing power per year
- Actionable step: Open a brokerage account and set up auto-invest in a broad index fund (reference Video #01)

5. HABIT #4: TRACK EVERY DOLLAR - KNOW YOUR NUMBERS (4:30-5:45)
- Rich habit: Know exactly where every dollar goes, review finances weekly
- Broke habit: Avoid looking at bank statements, no idea where money goes
- Research: 93% of wealthy people track their spending vs. 23% of those struggling financially
- Key insight: You can't optimize what you don't measure
- The awareness effect: Studies show simply tracking expenses reduces overspending by 15-20%
- Example: Someone who discovers they spend $400/month on food delivery without realizing it
- Actionable step: Download a free tracking app or use a spreadsheet; review every Sunday for 10 minutes

6. HABIT #5: AVOID LIFESTYLE INFLATION (5:45-7:00)
- Rich habit: Keeping lifestyle costs flat as income grows, investing the difference
- Broke habit: Upgrading everything (car, apartment, clothes, dining) with every raise
- Research: Parkinson's Law applied to money - expenses expand to fill available income unless constrained
- Key insight: The gap between income and expenses is your wealth-building engine
- Statistic: 60% of people earning over $100k still live paycheck to paycheck (due to lifestyle inflation)
- Example: Two people both get a $10k raise - one saves $8k of it, the other gets a nicer apartment and car
- The "lifestyle creep" trap: small upgrades ($5 coffee daily, subscription stacking, premium everything)
- Actionable step: Write down your current monthly expenses and commit to keeping them flat for 12 months

7. HABIT #6: BUILD MULTIPLE INCOME STREAMS (7:00-8:15)
- Rich habit: Developing 2-3+ sources of income (investments, side business, rental income)
- Broke habit: Relying 100% on a single paycheck from one employer
- Research: 65% of self-made millionaires had at least 3 income streams before reaching millionaire status
- Key insight: Multiple streams provide security AND accelerate wealth building
- Types of income: earned, portfolio (dividends/capital gains), passive (rental, royalties, digital products)
- Example: Someone with a $60k salary + $500/month dividends + $1,000/month side business = $79k total
- Actionable step: Identify ONE skill you can monetize outside your job; start building stream #2 this month

8. HABIT #7: INVEST IN FINANCIAL EDUCATION (8:15-9:30)
- Rich habit: Continuously learning about money, investing, taxes, and wealth building
- Broke habit: Avoiding financial topics, saying "I'm just not good with money"
- Research: 88% of wealthy people read educational material for 30+ minutes daily (Rich Habits Study)
- Key insight: Financial literacy is the meta-skill that makes all other habits possible
- The compound effect of knowledge: one insight can save/earn you thousands over a lifetime
- Example: Learning about tax-advantaged accounts can save $5,000-$15,000 per year in taxes
- Actionable step: Commit to 15 minutes of financial education daily (books, podcasts, channels like this one)

9. THE COMPOUND EFFECT OF ALL 7 HABITS (9:30-10:15)
- How these 7 habits work together synergistically
- The flywheel effect: each habit reinforces the others
- Timeline perspective: small daily habits create massive wealth gaps over 10-20 years
- Statistic: The average millionaire took 28 years to reach $1M (it's a habit game, not a lottery)
- Visual: Show the diverging wealth paths of "habit practitioners" vs. "non-practitioners" over 20 years

- Week 2: Review spending and identify one area of lifestyle inflation to cut
