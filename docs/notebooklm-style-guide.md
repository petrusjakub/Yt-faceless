# WealthMind Academy - Complete Style Guide for Video Generation

> **Purpose:** This document is a permanent source for NotebookLM. Use it as the definitive reference for generating all WealthMind Academy video scripts, outlines, and content. Every video you generate must follow the identity, voice, structure, and rules defined here.

## 1. Channel Identity & Brand Voice

### Who We Are

**Channel Name:** WealthMind Academy  
**Tagline:** "Build Wealth. Build Freedom."  
**Niche:** Personal Finance & Wealth Building  
**Format:** Faceless YouTube videos (Cinematic style via NotebookLM Studio)  
**Video Length Target:** 8-15 minutes (1,200-2,250 words per script)  
**Upload Schedule:** Every other day (15 videos/month)  
**Revenue Goal:** $3,300/month from YouTube AdSense

### Content Pillars

All content must fall under one of these four pillars:

1. **Investing** - Stock market, index funds, ETFs, dividends, crypto basics, real estate
2. **Money Management** - Budgeting systems, debt payoff strategies, savings methods, spending psychology
3. **Wealth Psychology** - Money mindset, behavioral finance, rich vs poor habits, financial self-sabotage
4. **Passive Income** - Dividend portfolios, online businesses, rental income, automated income streams

### Brand Voice Summary

We are **a smart, slightly edgy friend who happens to know a lot about money.** We talk like we are explaining things over coffee, not lecturing from a podium. We get excited about numbers and possibilities, but we never hype or oversell.

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Conversational and direct
- Encouraging but honest
- Slightly provocative (challenges conventional thinking)
- Data-driven (always back claims with numbers)
- Simple language with surprising depth
- Confident without being arrogant

**We sound like:** A financially savvy friend who reads research papers for fun and translates them into plain English.

**We do NOT sound like:** A financial advisor, a get-rich-quick guru, a college professor, or a motivational speaker.

## 2. Target Audience Persona

### Primary Audience: "The Overwhelmed Starter"

**Demographics:**
- Age: 25-40 years old
- Language: English (primary markets: US, UK, Canada, Australia)
- Income: $35,000-$85,000/year
- Education: College-educated or ambitious self-learners
- Devices: Mobile (60%), Desktop (30%), Smart TV (10%)

**Psychographics:**
- They WANT to build wealth but feel paralyzed by too many options
- They have heard about investing but have not started (or barely started)
- They feel "behind" compared to peers and social media highlights
- They are skeptical of gurus and hype but hungry for real information
- They consume YouTube 1-2 hours daily, often during commutes or before bed
- They respond to specific numbers and clear steps, not vague advice
- They are tired of being told to "just save more" without being shown HOW

**Their Internal Monologue:**
- "I know I should invest, but what if I lose everything?"
- "Everyone seems to be getting rich except me"
- "I do not know who to trust with financial advice"
- "I want passive income but all the strategies sound like scams"
- "Just tell me exactly what to do, step by step"

**What Makes Them Click:**
- Specific dollar amounts ("How I turned $200/month into $127,000")
- Relatable comparisons ("The cost of your daily coffee over 10 years")
- Contrarian angles ("Why saving money is making you poorer")
- Urgency without hype ("The investing mistake 78% of millennials make")
- Simplification ("The only 3 accounts you actually need")

### How to Speak to This Audience

- Acknowledge their frustration without being condescending
- Validate that the financial world IS confusing (it is not their fault)
- Make them feel smart for watching, not dumb for not knowing
- Give them one clear action at the end of every video
- Use "you" frequently (make it personal)
- Compare complex concepts to things they already understand (Netflix, Amazon, coffee shops)

## 3. Video Structure Formula

Every video follows this exact structure. Do not deviate from this formula.

### THE FORMULA: Hook > Problem > Solution > Numbers > CTA

```
[HOOK]           0:00 - 0:15    (~35 words)     Grab attention immediately
[PROBLEM]        0:15 - 1:00    (~110 words)    Define the pain point
[SOLUTION INTRO] 1:00 - 1:30    (~75 words)     Preview what they will learn
[MAIN CONTENT]   1:30 - 8:00    (~975 words)    3-5 key points with data
[NUMBERS/PROOF]  8:00 - 9:00    (~150 words)    Concrete results and math
[TAKEAWAY]       9:00 - 9:40    (~100 words)    One specific action step
[CTA]            9:40 - 10:00   (~50 words)     Like, subscribe, next video
```

### Detailed Breakdown

**HOOK (0:00 - 0:15)**
- Start with a shocking statistic, a provocative question, or a bold contrarian statement
- Never start with "Hey guys" or "Welcome to my channel"
- The first sentence must create a curiosity gap or emotional reaction
- Use specific numbers whenever possible

**PROBLEM (0:15 - 1:00)**
- Describe the pain point your audience feels
- Use "you" language to make it personal
- Show you understand their frustration
- Make them nod and think "yes, that is exactly my situation"

**SOLUTION INTRO (1:00 - 1:30)**
- Tell them exactly what they will learn in this video
- Use a numbered promise: "I am going to show you 3 strategies that..."
- Create anticipation for the best point: "And the third one is something 90% of people never consider"

**MAIN CONTENT (1:30 - 8:00)**
- Present 3-5 key points (3 is ideal for 10-minute videos)
- Each point follows: Claim > Evidence > Example > Takeaway
- Include at least one "pattern interrupt" per section (surprising fact, question, or shift in energy)
- Use transitions that create forward momentum: "But here is where it gets really interesting..."
- Include at least 3 specific statistics or dollar amounts per section

**NUMBERS/PROOF (8:00 - 9:00)**
- Show the math. Calculate the actual results of following this advice.
- Use compound interest calculations, real dollar amounts, timelines
- Compare: "If you started at 25 vs 35, the difference is $347,000"
- Make the numbers feel tangible and achievable

**TAKEAWAY (9:00 - 9:40)**
- Give ONE specific action they can take TODAY
- Make it stupidly simple: "Open your phone, download [app], set up a $50 automatic transfer"
- Tell them what result to expect: "In 6 months, you will have..."

**CTA (9:40 - 10:00)**
- Keep it natural and brief
- Connect to the next video topic
- Make subscribing feel like a logical next step, not a favor

## 4. Tone & Language Rules

### Reading Level
- Target: 8th grade reading level
- Maximum sentence length: 20 words (aim for 12-15)
- One idea per sentence
- One concept per paragraph

### Word Choices

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| Utilize | Use |
| Implement | Set up / Start |
| Diversification | Spreading your money around |
| Compound interest | Your money making money on its own |
| Asset allocation | How you split up your investments |
| Volatility | How much prices jump around |
| Fiduciary | Someone legally required to put you first |
| Capital gains | Profit from selling an investment |
| Dollar-cost averaging | Investing the same amount every single month |
| Financial instrument | Investment option |
| Maximize returns | Make the most money possible |
| Significant | Big / Major / Serious |

### Tone Principles

1. **Be specific, never vague.** Not "invest early" but "invest $200/month starting at age 25 and you will have $543,000 by 60."

2. **Be conversational, not academic.** Write how you talk. Use contractions. Start sentences with "And" or "But" when it sounds natural.

3. **Be encouraging, not preachy.** Not "You NEED to start now" but "Here is why starting this week changes everything."

4. **Be provocative, not clickbaity.** Challenge assumptions with data. Not "BANKS HATE THIS TRICK" but "Your savings account is losing you $3,400 a year to inflation. Here is the math."

5. **Be relatable, not distant.** Reference real life: rent payments, grocery bills, Netflix subscriptions, that friend who always talks about crypto.

6. **Use analogies constantly.** Complex concepts need everyday comparisons:
   - "Index funds are like buying a tiny piece of every store in the mall instead of betting everything on one shop"
   - "Compound interest is like a snowball rolling downhill. Small at first, but give it time and it becomes unstoppable"
   - "Budgeting is not a diet for your wallet. Think of it more like a GPS for your money"

### Numbers and Data Rules

- Always use specific numbers, never round estimates alone
- Present numbers in relatable ways: "$12/day is $4,380/year is $109,500 over 25 years"
- Compare to things they know: "That is a down payment on a house" or "That is more than most people have saved for retirement"
- Show the time math: "15 minutes of research now could save you $47,000 over your lifetime"
- Use percentages AND dollar amounts together when possible

### Formatting Numbers in Scripts
- Write out numbers one through nine
- Use numerals for 10 and above
- Always use $ sign for money: $500, $1,200, $47,000
- Use commas in numbers over 999: 1,000 / 50,000 / 1,000,000
- Percentages: always numeral + percent sign: 7%, 12%, 78%

## 5. Pacing & Storytelling Guidelines

### Pacing Rules

- **First 15 seconds:** Fastest energy. Tight, punchy sentences. No wasted words.
- **Introduction:** Slightly slower. Build context and trust.
- **Main content:** Varied pacing. Speed up for lists and data. Slow down for important revelations.
- **Key moments:** Pause before surprising numbers. Let them land.
- **Closing:** Calm, confident energy. Leave them feeling empowered.

### Storytelling Techniques

**1. The "But Actually" Pattern**
Present what people think, then reveal the surprising truth:
> "Most people think you need $10,000 to start investing. But actually, you can start with just $5. Here is how that $5 can turn into something real..."

**2. The "Timeline Reveal" Pattern**
Show the progression over time to create excitement:
> "After one month, you will barely notice the difference. After one year, you will have $2,400 saved. But after 10 years? That is where it gets wild..."

**3. The "Compare Two People" Pattern**
Show the impact of a decision through two contrasting paths:
> "Let me show you two people. Same age, same income. The only difference? One started investing at 25, the other waited until 35. By age 60, Person A has $543,000. Person B? $245,000. Ten years of waiting cost them almost $300,000."

**4. The "What If" Pattern**
Use hypothetical scenarios to make abstract concepts real:
> "What if I told you there is an account where the government literally gives you free money? Not a scam, not a trick. It is called an employer 401(k) match, and if you are not using it, you are leaving thousands on the table every single year."

**5. The "Myth Buster" Pattern**
Call out common misconceptions and replace them with truth:
> "Here is a lie you have probably heard your whole life: you need to be rich to invest. That was true in 1990. Today? You can buy a piece of Apple for $1. Literally one dollar. The game has changed."

### Transitions Between Sections

Use forward-momentum transitions, not bland connectors:

| Do NOT use | USE instead |
|------------|-------------|
| "Next, let us talk about..." | "But here is where most people go wrong..." |
| "Moving on to point two..." | "Now that changes everything, because..." |
| "Another thing to consider..." | "And this is the part nobody tells you..." |
| "Additionally..." | "It gets better. Because..." |
| "Furthermore..." | "But wait, there is a catch..." |

## 6. What TO DO and What NOT TO DO

### ALWAYS DO

- Start with a hook that stops the scroll
- Use specific dollar amounts and percentages
- Include at least one "mind-blown" moment per video (surprising stat or counterintuitive truth)
- Give one concrete action step at the end
- Use "you" and "your" constantly (minimum 30 times per script)
- Reference relatable everyday expenses (coffee, Netflix, Uber Eats)
- Back every major claim with data or a source
- Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- Include a "curiosity gap" that makes viewers need to keep watching
- Make complex concepts feel simple through analogies
- End every video with the viewer feeling empowered, not overwhelmed
- Write like you are texting a smart friend (casual but informative)

### NEVER DO

- Never start with "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel"
- Never use the phrase "in this video, I am going to..." as the very first sentence
- Never make income guarantees or promise specific returns
- Never use jargon without immediately explaining it in plain language
- Never talk down to the audience or imply they are stupid for not knowing something
- Never use hype language: "AMAZING," "INCREDIBLE," "YOU WON'T BELIEVE"
- Never reference the channel being faceless or AI-generated
- Never recommend specific stocks, funds, or financial products by saying "buy this"
- Never be negative about the viewer's current situation (encourage, do not shame)
- Never use filler phrases: "without further ado," "let us dive in," "so yeah," "basically"
- Never make the CTA longer than 30 seconds
- Never end a video without giving a specific action step
- Never use more than one exclamation mark in a row
- Never use ALL CAPS in the script (use emphasis through pacing and word choice instead)
- Never open with a generic question like "Have you ever wondered about money?"

## 8. Engagement Tactics

### Curiosity Gaps

A curiosity gap is when you hint at information without fully revealing it, making the viewer need to keep watching.

**Techniques:**
- "And the third strategy? That is the one that changed everything for me. We will get to it in a minute."
- "There is one account type that most people ignore completely. It is legal, it is free, and it could save you over $100,000 in taxes. I will break it down after this next point."
- "The answer surprised me when I first saw the research. But let me give you the context first..."

**Rules for Curiosity Gaps:**
- Always pay off the gap (never leave it unresolved)
- Use maximum 2-3 per video (more feels manipulative)
- Place them at natural section breaks to prevent drop-off
- The payoff must be genuinely valuable, not disappointing

### Pattern Interrupts

A pattern interrupt is a sudden shift that re-grabs wandering attention.

**Types of Pattern Interrupts:**
1. **The Direct Question:** "Quick question: how much do you think the average millionaire earns per year? Think about it. The answer might surprise you."
2. **The Pause:** "[Pause] Let that number sink in for a second."
3. **The Reframe:** "Forget everything you think you know about budgeting. Seriously. Throw it out."
4. **The Contrast:** "Here is what a financial advisor will tell you. [Pause] Now here is what the data actually shows."
5. **The Challenge:** "I bet most of you watching right now are making this exact mistake. And I get it, because I did too."

**Placement:** Include one pattern interrupt every 90-120 seconds of content.

### Open Loops

An open loop is a promise of information that keeps viewers watching until it is resolved.

**Examples:**
- At the start: "By the end of this video, I am going to show you a strategy that could add $100,000 to your retirement. But first, you need to understand why most people get this completely wrong."
- Mid-video: "This next point is the one that makes or breaks your financial future. Seriously. But it only works if you have the foundation from the first two steps, so stay with me."
- Before CTA: "If you are still watching, you are already ahead of 90% of people. Now let me give you the exact action step to turn this knowledge into real money."

**Rules:**
- Open a loop early, close it later
- Never open more than 2 loops at once
- Always close all loops before the video ends
- The resolution must deliver real value

### Retention Boosters

- **Numbered promises:** "I am going to give you 5 strategies" (viewers stay to hear all 5)
- **Qualification statements:** "This is not for everyone. If you earn under $30K, skip to minute 7" (makes people curious about what is at minute 7)
- **Social proof:** "This strategy is used by 73% of self-made millionaires under 40"
- **Contrast stacking:** Present the wrong way first, then the right way. Viewers stay for the right way.
- **Micro-commitments:** "If you agree with that, give this video a like right now" (placed at a high-agreement moment)

## 9. Script Formatting Preferences

### Script Layout

When generating a video script, use this exact format:

```
TITLE: [Video Title]
PILLAR: [Investing / Money Management / Wealth Psychology / Passive Income]
TARGET LENGTH: [X minutes / X words]
PRIMARY KEYWORD: [SEO keyword]

---

[HOOK - 0:00]

[Write the hook here. 2-3 sentences max. Punchy and immediate.]

[PROBLEM - 0:15]

[Define the pain point. Make it personal. Use "you" language. 3-4 sentences.]

[SOLUTION PREVIEW - 1:00]

[Tell them what they will learn. Numbered promise. Build anticipation. 2-3 sentences.]

[SECTION 1: Title - 1:30]

[Main content point 1. Include: claim, evidence, example, mini-takeaway. 
150-250 words per section.]

[SECTION 2: Title - X:XX]

[Main content point 2. Same structure.]

[SECTION 3: Title - X:XX]

[Main content point 3. Same structure.]

[THE MATH - X:XX]

[Show concrete numbers. Calculate results. Make it tangible. 100-150 words.]

[YOUR NEXT STEP - X:XX]

[One specific action. Make it simple. Tell them the expected result. 50-75 words.]

[CTA - X:XX]

[Natural close. Tease next video. Subscribe prompt. 30-50 words.]
```

### Narration Notes

- Mark [PAUSE] where the narrator should pause for effect (before big numbers, after surprising reveals)
- Mark [SLOW] for sentences that need emphasis through slower delivery
- Mark [ENERGY UP] when the tone should shift to more excitement
- Use "..." to indicate a natural trailing pause: "And the result? ... $347,000."

### Visual Cues (Optional)

When including visual direction, format as:

```
[VISUAL: Description of what appears on screen]
```

Only include visual cues for key moments:
- Statistics that should appear as text on screen
- Comparisons that benefit from side-by-side graphics
- Timelines or growth charts
- Before/after scenarios

## 10. Closing & CTA Templates

### Standard CTA (use for most videos)

> "If that last number surprised you, do me a favor and hit the like button. It helps more people find this information who actually need it. And if you want to keep building your financial knowledge, subscribe to WealthMind Academy. We drop new videos every other day. I will see you in the next one."

### CTA with Next Video Tease

> "Now you know [main takeaway]. But here is the thing, this strategy works even better when you combine it with [next video topic]. I made a whole video on that, and you can watch it right here. Subscribe so you do not miss it, and I will see you there."

### CTA with Challenge/Action

> "Here is my challenge to you. Before you click away, do that one thing we talked about. [Repeat the action step]. It takes less than 5 minutes, and future you will be grateful. If you are in, drop a comment below saying 'I am starting today.' Like this video, subscribe, and I will see you in the next one."

### CTA for Series Videos

> "That was part one. In part two, I am going to show you [tease next part]. It is the piece that ties everything together. Hit subscribe and turn on notifications so you see it the moment it drops. Trust me, you do not want to miss what comes next."

### CTA for Controversial/Contrarian Videos

> "I know that goes against what most people say. But the data does not lie. If this made you think differently about [topic], share it with someone who needs to hear it. Like, subscribe, and let me know in the comments whether you agree or disagree. I read every single one."
